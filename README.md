## The Honest Risk Section

Audifort markets itself for a real and under-addressed problem. Tinnitus is genuinely difficult to treat, and many people feel dismissed when they're told to "just live with it." That frustration is valid.

But a few things are worth holding alongside that:

**What Audifort cannot do:** It cannot regenerate cochlear hair cells that have been permanently damaged. No supplement currently can. If you have significant, medically confirmed hearing loss, this product is not a substitute for medical evaluation or hearing aids.

**Potential interactions:** Grape Seed Extract may have mild blood-thinning properties. Gymnema Sylvestre and Green Tea Extract can interact with blood pressure and blood sugar medications. If you're on any medication, check with your doctor before starting.

**"Natural" does not mean side-effect-free.** Stomach upset (especially if taken without food) is the most commonly mentioned mild effect in reviews that discuss side effects at all.

**The 90-day money-back guarantee has a catch:** It covers the product cost, but not shipping and handling. If you're in the US and bought the 3- or 6-bottle package, return shipping is your responsibility. Factor that in if you're uncertain.

**Third-party listings are not safe.** The manufacturer is explicit: Amazon, eBay, or any unauthorized site may be selling counterfeit products. If you decide to purchase, do so from the official page only—and keep your order confirmation.
